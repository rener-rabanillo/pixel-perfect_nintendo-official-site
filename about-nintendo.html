Presidents

Nintendo was founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi, who initially focused on producing handmade Hanafuda playing cards. His dedication to quality and innovation established Nintendo as a leading brand in Japan's playing card market, setting the stage for its future success in entertainment. By the time he retired in 1929, the company was well-positioned for growth.

The torch was passed on to Sekiryo Kaneda, he married Fusajiro's daughter, Tei Yamauchi, and took on the Yamauchi surname. He modernized Nintendo's operations and transformed it into a limited partnership company. His efforts went to stabilize the company financially and ensured it was prepared for further expansions.

After Kaneda's passing in 1949, Fusajiro's great-grandson Hiroshi Yamauchi took over the company and transformed Nintendo from producing playing cards to developing and making video games. Hiroshi oversaw the creation of Nintendo's most iconic franchises, including Super Mario Bros., and The Legend of Zelda. He also introduced the revolutionary consoles such as the NES, SNES, and Game Boy.

In 2002, Satoru Iwata became the first president not from the Yamauchi family. Known for his developer background as a video game programmer and designer. His approach onto the modern age focused on creating accessible, family-friendly gaming experiences. In his tenure, Nintendo achieved groundbreaking success with the Nintendo DS and WII.

After Iwata's untimely death in 2015, Tatsumi Kimishima stepped in to provide stability during critical times for the company. His leadership saw the launch of the Nintendo Switch, which revitalized Nintendo's console market dominance and became one of the best-selling consoles in history. He also expanded Nintendo's presence in the mobile gaming industry and set the stage for the company's next phase of growth.

Since 2018, Shuntaro Furukawa has served as Nintendo's president, focusing on maintaining the strong momentum of the Nintendo Switch, ensuring its global success through a consistent stream of high-quality games and innovative accessories like the Nintendo Labo and the Ring Fit Adventure. He has spearheaded Nintendo's efforts to expand beyond traditional gaming.

History

Beginnings (1889-1929)

Fusajiro Yamauchi founded Nintendo as Nintendo Koppai in Kyoto, Japan, as a hanafuda playing card manufacturer, and were sold in dedicated Nintendo stores in Kyoto and Osaka. This achievement led Nintendo to become the first Japanese company to find success in manufacturing and selling western-style playing cards.

However, in 1929, Fusajiro Yamauchi retired and left the company to his son-in-law, Sekiryo Kaneda.

New successor (1929-1949)

In his tenure, Kaneda set up a joint partnership company named Yamauchi Nintendo & Co., modernizing operations and transforming Nintendo into a special partnership company. A separate distribution company for the range of Western playing cards was set up, named Marufuku Co. Ltd.

In 1949, he passed away and Fusajiro's great-grandson, Hiroshi Yamauchi, took over the company.

Venturing into the video game world (1949-2002)

Initially, Nintendo was still focused on producing hanafuda playing cards well into the 20th century, but Yamauchi sought to expand the business beyond playing cards. During the 1950s and 1960s, he experimented with various industries, including toys, instant rice, and even love hotels, though many of these ventures failed. But success came when Nintendo ventured into toys and later, the rising video game industry. Under his leadership, the company produced groundbreaking games such as Donkey Kong, Super Mario Bros. and consoles including the Super Famicom in 1983 and the Nintendo Entertainment System in 1985.

In 2002, he stepped down as president of the company and handed the reins to Satoru Iwata.

A new era (2002-2015)

Under his Iwata's tenure, Nintendo achieved groundbreaking success with the Nintendo Wii and the 3DS, significantly expanding the gaming audience. Known for his hands-on approach as he was a former video game programmer and developer, he was deeply involved in game development and famously worked on titles like PokÃ©mon Gold and Silver to troubleshoot programming issues. Iwata's philosophy of "games for everyone" and his approachable demeanor made him beloved by fans and colleagues alike.

Into the Future (2018-Present)

Since then, Nintendo has continued to build on his legacy, embracing innovation and creativity to remain a dominant force in the video game industry. The launch of the Nintendo Switch in 2017 revolutionized the industry by combining handheld and home console gaming capabilities. Its versatility and expansive library of games, including major franchises like The Legend of Zelda, Super Mario, and Animal Crossing, have made it one of the best-selling consoles of all time.

Nintendo has also embraced digital gaming with the Nintendo eShop, allowing players to access classic titles, indie games, and new releases seamlessly. Today, Nintendo continues to balance innovation and imagination with their iconic titles and consoles, providing fun and entertainment for all ages alike.
